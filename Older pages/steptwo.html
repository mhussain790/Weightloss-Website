Now, let's set a few goals:


Ultimate Goal

What is your ultimate goal? In other words, where do you see yourself weight-wise at the end of your journey or after a period of time. For example, I'm at 195 lbs right now and I'd like to lose 25 lbs by the end of 4 months. So my Ultimate Goal would be:

Ultimate Goal: LOSE 25 lbs in 4 months.

Goal #1

Now let's set a smaller goal that's easier to reach. If you just focus on your end goal then you might get lost a long the way if you don't see results fast enough. So by having smaller goals, they fuel us into pushing ourselves more and more. That feeling of accomplishment after meeting a goal is really one of the most important factors in helping us complete our journey.

Personally, I'd like to gain flexibility to where I can bend down and touch my toes. So my goal will be to touch my toes and hold that position for 60 seconds without too much discomfort. I recommend choosing something feasible for this goal. Something that's not too far out or will surprise you by taking longer than expected.

Goal #1: Forward bend to touch toes. Holding for 60 seconds. Time: 2 weeks!

Why 2 weeks?


This part relies on you. You know your body better than anyone so I won't be able to tell you how long to set your time limit to. If you're starting out then I'd say think of a time limit and add a little bit more on to it, just to be safe.

Goal #2

So this is going to be a simpler goal. For this one I want you to choose a weight and a date you'd like to hit that weight at. For example, I'm starting out wanting to lose weight so I'd like to lose about 3 pounds by the end of week 2. I recommend staying between 3-5 lbs just because you'll be surprised at how much weight you'll lose if you stick to your diet for 2 weeks. I use the 2 week period to tell:

1) If my diet is working.

  • If not then I need to adjust this through nutrition or excercise

    • 2) How far did I progress?
