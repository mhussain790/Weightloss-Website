Counting calories burned!

How do you count calories burned from exercise?

Cardio machines can usually give a rough estimate of how many calories are burned while working out. You can even enter your age and weight to get a closer estimation, but how do you count calories elsewhere such as after lifting weights, playing basketball, or even swimming?

Devices such as smart watches or fitness tracking watches can count your activity and calories. Some examples of these are the FitBit, Polar Sports Watch, and even the Apple Watch.



From our experience, we love the Polar M400 Smart watch. First, you enter your personal information in such as height and weight. It takes readings according to your body so it should give you a more accurate estimate than a Cardio Machine. Also, not only does it function as a watch, but it also shows notifications from texts or calls received by your phone. You can also see your different heart rate levels, your locations such as how far you went such as on a run with the GPS tracking ability of the watch. Furthermore, if you use the heart rate monitor than it gets a more accurate reading of your activity.

How comfortable is it?

After almost 2 years of use, we haven't had any comfortability issues with our M400. Both the watch and heart rate monitor aren't uncomfortable to wear. The M400's straps are rubber-like so it doesn't cut into your skin even during movement. Initally, we thought the heart rate monitor would be uncomfortable, but it hasn't caused any problems and we tend to forget about import while working out. Finally, this watch is waterproof. We've forgot to take our watch off during showers and baths many many times. We've also wore it while swimming with no problems!

There are a couple of other Polar Smart Watch products out there that you can check out. We recommend looking at reviews online and even checking out YouTube videos to see how they perform.
