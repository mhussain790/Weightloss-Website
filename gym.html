Should you go to the Gym?

One thing we recommend for you is to get a gym membership. There are so many gyms out there today and each one provides a unique experience. Some are more catered to one need or another.

Why do we recommend a Gym membership?

Unless you’ve got your own gym equipment, cardio machines, or even a set of weights at home then you’re somewhat limited in what you can do. Also, unless you have the self-discipline to be able to push yourself to workout at home then getting some type of exercise will be even harder to do.


The gym is not the only place to be able to get a good workout in. We've put together a list of gym alternatives we've had experience with. Click the button to see the list!

We recommend getting a gym membership because for around $25+/month you can get access to a variety of equipment that’ll cost thousands of dollars to buy on your own. You can buy your own cardio machine and maybe a bench, or a set of weights, but what if you can’t push yourself to workout at home? Pushing yourself to go to the gym allows you to leave the house. Put some headphones on and hit a cardio machine or maybe lift some weights. If you aren't comfortable lifting free weights such as dumbells, bench pressing, squating in the squat rack, etc., many gyms have a lot of weight machines that are safer and newbie friendlyl. If your gym has a pool or a basketball court then you have access to those as well.

Furthermore, you can meet some great people at the gym. Maybe a life partner or friends who enjoys the same passion as you.

Here is our recommendation list:

    Planet Fitness


    Pro’s

  • Decent Price: ~$20 - $25
  • Open 24 hrs
  • Focused on beginners/newbies to exercise
  • Many cardio machines
  • A lot of machines for lifting
  • These come with a lot of diagrams showing how each exercise is done

    Con’s

  • Not really a place for experienced or advanced weight lifters
  • Only have Smith Machines
  • No bench press or squat racks
  • Dumbbells only go up to about 60-70 lbs
  • Gym gets crowded around 6-8PM

    Lifetime Fitness


    Pro's

  • Expensive: $60+ a month
  • Open 24 hrs
  • Has everything you could imagine
  • Sauna
  • Pool
  • Complete set of benches, squat racks, weights, etc.
  • Basketball Court
  • Different type of classes
  • Yoga
  • Strength
  • Kickboxing
  • Pilates

    Con’s

  • Expensive

There are other gyms such as Anytime Fitness, 24hr Fitness, Crunch Fitness, etc. We recommend checking Yelp, but take each review with a grain of salt.
